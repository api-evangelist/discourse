<img alt="Every Language is Welcome" src="https://storage.ghost.io/c/7d/70/7d70d59c-7408-4583-b44d-98a43cdfa8fd/content/images/2026/03/Discourse-Header-Images--11-.png" /><p><em>Hey all &#x1f44b;</em></p><p>I&apos;ve been spending a lot of time on multilingual support at Discourse lately, and the more I dig in, the more I realise how much there is to do. I wanted to share why we care about this so much and what&apos;s coming.</p><p>The short version of what we believe at Discourse: a community&#x2019;s language should not be the factor that stops someone from participating in a community. Whether your forum runs in English, Spanish, Portuguese, or Mandarin, there will always be people who want to join but don&apos;t speak that language fluently.</p><h2 id="this-isnt-new-for-us"><strong>This isn&apos;t new for us</strong></h2><p>We built i18n into Discourse in 2012, our first year, and community members were already advocating for proper internationalization back in 2013. One of them said it best: <em>&quot;Give internationalization proper love and the product will spread across the world.&quot;</em> We listened, and they were right.</p><p>Since then we&apos;ve gone from basic locale files to Crowdin, from a community translation plugin to official AI-powered translation, and from &quot;we should probably do this&quot; to a dedicated project team working on it full-time. It&apos;s been a long road and we&apos;re still on it.</p><h2 id="the-thing-that-made-it-urgent"><strong>The thing that made it urgent</strong></h2><p>Communities have always been global, even when their topics aren&apos;t. An open-source project attracts contributors who speak Portuguese, Turkish, Polish. A gaming community draws players whose first language is Arabic or Mandarin. Even our own team at Discourse spans 24 countries and speaks over a dozen languages. We don&apos;t need to imagine a multilingual community, we are one. We&apos;ve heard from customers for years, from international NGOs in Sub-Saharan Africa, to gaming companies in Nigeria, asking us to help them reach people in their language.</p><p>But it got real for us in late 2024 when Gerhard (Discourse engineer!) showed that Discourse.org was invisible in German Google search results &#x1f640; If someone searching in German couldn&apos;t find <em>us</em>, how many communities were invisible to their own potential members?</p><p>That was the &quot;oh no&quot; moment that turned conviction into action.</p><h2 id="a-number-that-surprised-us"><strong>A number that surprised us</strong></h2><p>We&#x2019;ve looked at the browser language settings of visitors to meta.discourse.org, our (primarily) English-language support forum over a 30-day window.</p><p><strong>36% of visitors had a non-English primary language.</strong> Over 80 distinct languages. Chinese, German, French, Spanish, Russian, Japanese, Portuguese... and a long tail of dozens more.</p><p>The gap between the language a community operates in and the languages its people actually speak is where participation and equitable community ownership dies. Every person who can&apos;t comfortably read or write in the forum&apos;s language represents&#xa0; a question that doesn&apos;t get asked, and a contribution that doesn&apos;t get made.</p><h2 id="whats-coming"><strong>What&apos;s coming</strong></h2><p>This is the first in a series I&apos;m calling <em>Discourse Without Borders</em>. Over the next few posts I&#x2019;ll aim to cover:</p><ul><li><strong>How we built multilingual support into the platform</strong> - from a community-contributed plugin in 2013 to AI-powered translation today</li><li><strong>What it&apos;s actually like to use Discourse in another language</strong> - data from our own community, honest about what works and what doesn&apos;t</li><li><strong>A practical playbook for structuring a multilingual community</strong> - the models, the tradeoffs and the setup</li><li><strong>Real examples of communities getting this right</strong></li></ul><p>I&apos;m genuinely excited about this series. And if you&apos;re running a community (or thinking of building&#xa0; one) and are wondering whether multilingual support matters for you, I think the data will surprise you. It certainly surprised us!</p><p>We would love to hear from folks who are already navigating this. What&apos;s working? What&apos;s painful? Drop a reply or find me on meta!</p><hr /><p><em>Next up: From Plugin to Platform - how Discourse built multilingual support over 13 years (coming soon)</em></p>
