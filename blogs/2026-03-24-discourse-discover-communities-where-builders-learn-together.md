Earn,&quot; a program that rewards experienced members for helping newcomers get unstuck, and the categories match how people actually use the tool: &quot;Questions&quot; handles most of the traffic, &quot;Help Me Build My Workflow&quot; is where members bring their ideas and sketch out solutions together, and &quot;Built with n8n&quot; is where people post finished automations. </p><div class="kg-card kg-button-card kg-align-center"><a class="kg-btn kg-btn-accent" href="https://community.n8n.io/?ref=blog.discourse.org">n8n on Discourse</a></div><h2 id="godot-forum">Godot forum</h2><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img alt="Discourse Discover: Communities where builders learn together" class="kg-image" height="1145" src="https://storage.ghost.io/c/7d/70/7d70d59c-7408-4583-b44d-98a43cdfa8fd/content/images/2026/03/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-6.48.30---am.png" width="2000" /></figure><p>Godot Engine has become one of the most popular open-source game engines, and its Discourse forum has the energy you&apos;d expect from a community passionate about building. On any given day the front page mixes technical troubleshooting (&quot;How do I make a player stand on moving platforms?&quot;), big-picture debate (&quot;Is the AI bubble about to burst?&quot;), and members sharing games in progress from 3D sidescrolling shooters to Sonic-inspired platformers...</p><div class="kg-card kg-button-card kg-align-center"><a class="kg-btn kg-btn-accent" href="https://forum.godotengine.org/?ref=blog.discourse.org">Godot on Discourse</a></div><h2 id="freecodecamp-forum">freeCodeCamp forum</h2><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img alt="Discourse Discover: Communities where builders learn together" class="kg-image" height="1195" src="https://storage.ghost.io/c/7d/70/7d70d59c-7408-4583-b44d-98a43cdfa8fd/content/images/2026/03/Screenshot-2026-03-24-at-6.49.04---am.png" width="2000" /></figure><p>When freeCodeCamp launched their forum on Discourse, they created a hub where questions are welcome. Their approach scaled, and the forum now pulls millions of views each month across categories like JavaScript (60,000+ topics), HTML-CSS (90,000+ topics), and Python (10,000+ topics), each one working as a reference library where years-old answers still get traffic because they hold up. </p><div class="kg-card kg-button-card kg-align-center"><a class="kg-btn kg-btn-accent" href="https://forum.freecodecamp.org/?ref=blog.discourse.org">freeCodeCamp on Discourse</a></div><hr /><div class="kg-card kg-callout-card kg-callout-card-grey"><div class="kg-callout-emoji">&#x1f50d;</div><div class="kg-callout-text"><a href="https://discover.discourse.com/?ref=blog.discourse.org" rel="noreferrer">Discourse Discover</a> highlights communities that show what&apos;s possible when you combine thoughtful platform design with engaged users. <br /><br />We&apos;re looking for forums that have built something interesting, whether that&apos;s a thriving knowledge base, an active developer community, or a space where meaningful conversations happen at scale. <br /><br />If you&apos;re running a Discourse community and you&apos;re ready for your close-up, reach out to us at marketing@discourse.org. <br /><br />We&apos;d love to hear what you&apos;re building!</div></div>
